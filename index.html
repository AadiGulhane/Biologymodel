📘 Key Concepts
① Photon Trapping Index (PTI)

The Photon Trapping Index measures how many times a ray interacts inside the nanostructure on average — before it either escapes or gets absorbed.

0.88
int/ray 0.88 interactions/ray — each photon undergoes 0.88 internal bounces on average before exiting. A PTI of 2.5 interactions/ray means photons bounce 2.5 times on average before escaping or being absorbed. Higher PTI = more absorption opportunities per photon.

⚠ PTI is NOT a percentage. It is a rate: average internal photon interactions per ray. It can exceed 1.0, 2.0, or even 5.0 if photons bounce many times. The Flat Silicon Baseline¹ always scores ≈ 0 because there is no multi-bounce confinement — only a single surface interaction.

Why it matters: higher PTI = each photon gets more chances to be absorbed = stronger structural efficiency in the Morpho design.

② Structural Color (Morpho Butterfly)

The vivid blue of a Morpho butterfly wing is not pigment. It comes entirely from nano-scale structures interacting with light.

  • The wing contains stacked thin-film lamellae only ~70–100 nm thick.
  • Light reflects from each layer. Waves from different layers add together or cancel (interference).
  • At ~450 nm (blue wavelength) the layers are spaced so reflected waves reinforce each other — producing brilliant blue.
  • At other angles, the path difference changes → color shifts. This is iridescence.

📌 This is the same principle used in anti-reflection coatings on camera lenses and soap bubbles.

③ Why Blue is Favoured

The lamella spacing (~70–100 nm) is tuned to constructively reflect wavelengths of 400–500 nm (the blue range). Other wavelengths are:

  • Absorbed — longer wavelengths (red, IR) pass through and are absorbed by the dark substrate beneath.
  • Scattered — mid-wavelengths are scattered at many angles and do not produce a strong peak.

For solar cells, this sharp blue-reflection dip is visible in the Absorptance vs Wavelength graph as a notch at ~450 nm in the Morpho curve.

④ Absorption · Reflection · Transmission
⬤ Absorption

Photon energy is converted to electrical energy (or heat) inside the silicon. This is what we want — more absorption = higher efficiency.

⬤ Reflection

Photon bounces back off the surface without entering the material. Fresnel reflection causes ~31% loss on bare silicon. Anti-reflection coatings (ARC) reduce this.

⬤ Transmission

Photon passes all the way through the panel without being absorbed. This is wasted energy — thin panels are especially susceptible.

Flat Surface

A smooth silicon surface reflects ~31% of incoming photons via Fresnel reflection. Single-pass absorption limits capture to 48–54% at typical sun angles. Transmitted photons are lost.

Morpho Butterfly Lattice

Vertical spines and tapered horizontal lamellae create enclosed cells. Photons bounce repeatedly — each bounce is another absorption opportunity. Multi-bounce trapping achieves >75% capture.
