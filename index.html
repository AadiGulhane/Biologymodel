Flat Surface

A smooth silicon surface reflects ~31% of incoming photons via Fresnel reflection. Single-pass absorption limits capture to 48–54% at typical sun angles. Transmitted photons are lost.

Morpho Butterfly Lattice

Vertical spines and tapered horizontal lamellae create enclosed cells. Photons bounce repeatedly — each bounce is another absorption opportunity. Multi-bounce trapping achieves >75% capture.

Absorption vs Wavelength (nm) — Morpho rhetenor Wing
Species: Morpho rhetenor — Its wing scales contain stacked thin-film lamellae that produce structural blue coloration through thin-film interference, causing a sharp reflectance peak near 450 nm (visible as the absorption dip in the purple curve). Outside this blue band, the multi-layer nanostructure enables high broadband absorption.
Sources: Vigneron et al. (2003) · Huang et al. (2007)
